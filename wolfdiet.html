Wolves are usually the apex predetor or top of the food chain wherever they go. They are carnivores and like to eat big hoofed mammals.
When they can't find big animals they eat smaller animals, like rabbits, mice, and beavers.
Adult wolves can eat about 20 pounds of meat in 1 meal.
Wolves' food depend on what species of wolves and their enviroment. In most habitats Wolves mostly eat elk, moose, and white-tail deer. 1 wolf can eat 15-20 WHOLE pack animals in a year.
In winter the wolves have less food. In summer and spring the wolves have a lot of prey because of all the animals being born. Wolves usually hunt older or younger prey because they are weaker and easier to catch.
